Creating a PGP Keypair

What happens when you create a new keypair in Kestrel Mail.

Choosing a password

When you create a keypair, you'll be asked to set a password (sometimes called a passphrase). This password protects your private key — without it, nobody can use your key to decrypt messages or sign emails on your behalf.

Choose something strong and memorable. You'll need this password whenever Kestrel Mail needs to decrypt an incoming message or sign an outgoing one.

Use a different password than your email account password. If your email password is ever compromised, your encryption keys will still be safe.

Picking a key size

You'll also choose a key size, measured in bits. A larger key is harder to crack but takes slightly longer to generate and use:

1024 bit

Fastest, but considered weak by modern standards. Not recommended for sensitive communication.

2048 bit

A good balance of security and speed. Suitable for most people.

4096 bit

Maximum security. Slightly slower to encrypt and decrypt, but practically unbreakable with current technology.

If you're unsure, 2048 bit is a solid default. You can always generate a new, stronger keypair later if your needs change.

What happens next

After you click Next, Kestrel Mail generates your keypair. You'll then be prompted to save a backup of your private key — this is an important step, because losing your private key means losing access to all messages encrypted with it. There is no recovery option.
