Saving Your Private Key

Why backing up your private key matters and how to do it safely.

If you lose your private key, every message encrypted with it becomes permanently unreadable. This includes messages you've received and copies of encrypted messages you've sent. There is no recovery option — not even Kestrel Mail can help you get them back.

How it works

When you choose to save your private key, Kestrel Mail exports your keypair as a standard file that you can store wherever you like. This file contains both your public and private keys, protected by the password you set during keypair creation.

Where to store your backup

Don't rely on a single copy. If the device it's stored on is lost, stolen, or damaged, your key goes with it. Keep multiple backups in different locations:

  • An encrypted USB drive stored somewhere safe
  • A secure cloud folder (encrypted or password-protected)
  • A second device you control, like a laptop or external hard drive

Can I save it later?

Yes. If you skip the backup during setup, you can always export your keys later from Kestrel Mail's account settings. But we strongly recommend saving a backup right away — it only takes a moment, and it protects you from a worst-case scenario that can't be undone.
